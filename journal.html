The Bleeding Heart

Why a flower whose blossoms are heart-shaped

The bleeding heart (Lamprocapnos spectabilis) was not chosen for marketing reasons. It was chosen because nature already drew it — a single bloom that holds the flower and the heart together, with a small pearl-shaped drop hanging beneath, as if the heart is releasing something precious. Heart, flower, and pearl, all in one natural form.

But the flower says more than its shape. The bleeding heart is the story of a woman who has lived — who has grown through chapters of love and loss, of being broken and being remade. The pearl that hangs from the bloom is the treasure that came from those chapters. The softness that arrives after the hardness. The elegance that is earned, not given.

That is why a pearl rests beneath every bleeding-heart piece in this collection. Not as decoration, but as a quiet acknowledgement: the things we wear can carry what we have lived.

The Bleeding Heart is the brand's permanent signature, and the first of many flowers Adornme will carry.

The Devotion Collection

For those who carry faith alongside their love story

The Devotion Collection was not planned. It grew out of pieces I kept making for myself — small reminders of grace, of being held by something larger. Christ, the cross, the heart that carries both — these are not statements. They are companions. For the woman who carries her faith quietly alongside her love story, who wants something close to her skin that knows her on the days that feel heavy.

More pieces will come, gently, as they are ready.

Founder's Letter

I made what I have become

I started Adornme as a reflection of my own becoming — a desire to wear the values, faith, love, and resilience that carried me through the chapters that shaped my life.

Adornme was drawn slowly, over years, in search of symbols I could not find anywhere else — pieces that carried memory, meaning, and emotional depth, while still inviting others to make them their own.

The bleeding heart that holds love, loss, and the growth that follows. The pearl born from pressure. The cross resting above an open heart. These were never meant to be statements. They became anchors.

Adornme is a symbolic jewelry house built from the belief that what we wear can quietly remind us who we are, what we hold sacred, and what we continue becoming.

The name beneath

Mehraein, the name I was born with

Mehraein is the name on my birth certificate. In Persian, it means the way of the sun, and the way of kindness — old words for an old idea, that warmth and gentleness are not weaknesses but the rarest forms of strength.

Most people know me as Mira. Mehraein is the longer name underneath — quieter, more private. When I named the brand, I did not name it after myself. But the brand's language — the warmth, the love, the choice to be kind — that came from this name.

Mehraein is the way Adornme was made.

The Stone Story

Why mother-of-pearl, why a pearl drop

Mother-of-pearl is what a pearl rests against before it is born. It is the soft, iridescent inside of an oyster shell — slower-formed than the pearl itself, and quieter. The Cuff uses two hearts of mother-of-pearl with a pearl drop beneath each. The bleeding-heart bloom releasing a pearl. A small story, told twice, in stone.

The Stone Story will continue. Future seasons may bring malachite, lapis, carnelian, turquoise — and future collections will bring new forms: a daisy, a sunflower, other flowers that carry their own stories. The Bleeding Heart is the first, but the language of the brand will keep growing.

Brand notes · May 2026
A Quiet Vow

$1 from every order

The first months of motherhood can be the loneliest months of a woman's life. The love is real, but so is the dark place that sometimes comes with it — and not everyone has someone to call.

Postpartum Support International was the only choice. The brand's themes — Divine Love, the women who love and are loved, the love that holds families together — are not separable from the mothers who need someone to hear them. One dollar from every piece goes to PSI. Quarterly reports are public.
